SCOBY Protection Mechanisms

  • Physical Barrier: The SCOBY forms a cellulose mat on the surface that physically blocks contaminants from entering the liquid.
  • Acidic Environment: The fermentation process creates an acidic environment (low pH) that inhibits the growth of many harmful organisms.
  • Competitive Exclusion: SCOBY bacteria and yeast consume available resources, making it difficult for contaminants to establish themselves.
  • Antimicrobial Compounds: The fermentation produces various compounds that have antimicrobial properties.
  • Oxygen Consumption: The SCOBY consumes oxygen, creating an anaerobic environment that inhibits aerobic contaminants like mold.

Mathematical Explanations

SCOBY Growth (Logistic Growth)

\[ \frac{dS}{dt} = r_s \cdot S \cdot (1 - \frac{S}{K}) \]

Where \(S\) is the SCOBY size, \(r_s\) is the growth rate, and \(K\) is the carrying capacity. This logistic equation models how the SCOBY grows over time, with growth slowing as the SCOBY approaches its carrying capacity.

pH Decrease (Acid Production)

\[ \frac{dpH}{dt} = -k_{pH} \cdot (S + B) \]

The pH drops as acids are produced by the SCOBY (\(S\)) and bacteria (\(B\)), with \(k_{pH}\) as the rate constant. The pH is bounded by a minimum value, reflecting the biological constraints of acid production.

Oxygen Consumption

\[ \frac{dO_2}{dt} = -k_{O_2} \cdot (S + B + Y) \]

Oxygen (\(O_2\)) is consumed by the SCOBY (\(S\)), bacteria (\(B\)), and yeast (\(Y\)) with rate constant \(k_{O_2}\). As oxygen decreases, the environment becomes less favorable for aerobic contaminants.

Beneficial Bacteria Growth

\[ \frac{dB}{dt} = r_b \cdot B \cdot g_{pH}(pH) \cdot g_{O_2}(O_2) \]

Bacteria (\(B\)) grow at rate \(r_b\), modified by growth factors that depend on pH (\(g_{pH}\)) and oxygen (\(g_{O_2}\)). Beneficial bacteria typically thrive in low-pH environments and can tolerate lower oxygen levels.

Beneficial Yeast Growth

\[ \frac{dY}{dt} = r_y \cdot Y \cdot g_{pH}(pH) \cdot g_{O_2}(O_2) \]

Yeast (\(Y\)) grows at rate \(r_y\), also modified by pH and oxygen-dependent growth factors. Yeasts have different optimal conditions compared to bacteria, creating a complex microbial ecosystem.

Contaminant Growth

\[ \frac{dC_i}{dt} = r_i \cdot C_i \cdot g_{pH}(pH) \cdot g_{O_2}(O_2) \cdot \frac{1}{1 + 0.1(B + Y)} \]

Contaminant \(i\) grows at its intrinsic rate \(r_i\), modified by pH and oxygen factors, and competitive exclusion. The competitive exclusion factor \(\frac{1}{1 + 0.1(B + Y)}\) reduces contaminant growth as beneficial microbes increase.

Physical Barrier (Contaminant Blocking Probability)

\[ P_{block} = \frac{S}{S + S_{half}} \]

The probability of the SCOBY blocking a contaminant increases with SCOBY size (\(S\)), where \(S_{half}\) is the SCOBY size at which the blocking probability is 50%.

pH Growth Factor for Beneficial Organisms

\[ g_{pH}(pH, threshold) = \begin{cases} 1.0 & \text{if } pH \leq threshold \\ \max(0.2, 1.0 - 0.3 \cdot (pH - threshold)) & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} \]

For beneficial organisms, growth is optimal at lower pH levels below a threshold, and decreases linearly as pH rises above that threshold.

pH Growth Factor for Contaminants

\[ g_{pH}(pH, threshold) = \begin{cases} 0.2 & \text{if } pH \leq threshold \\ \min(1.0, 0.2 + 0.8 \cdot \frac{pH - threshold}{7.0 - threshold}) & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} \]

Contaminants generally prefer higher pH levels, with growth severely inhibited below a threshold. Growth increases as pH rises above the threshold, reaching optimal levels in more neutral conditions.
